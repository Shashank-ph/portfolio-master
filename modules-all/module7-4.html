E-portfolio Activity - Research Proposal Review

  • Which of the methods described in this week's reading would you think would suit your purpose?
    Based on the literature review covering the period from 2000 to 2025, the most suitable method for addressing cyberbullying detection appears to be a hybrid approach combining deep learning (DL) and large language models (LLMs), such as fine-tuned BERT variants integrated with Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. This method aligns to achieve accurate F1-scores by capturing multimodal data (text, images, and videos) and contextual nuances, such as sarcasm or cultural slang. The hybrid model's ability to process diverse datasets from platforms like X and Instagram, while addressing biases through explainable AI, makes it ideal for real-time detection and prevention. Its scalability suits large-scale social media environments, striking a balance between accuracy and practical deployment.

    Which data collection methods would you consider using?
    For data collection, I would consider a combination of web scraping from public social media platforms (e.g., X, Instagram, and Facebook) to gather real-time posts, comments, and images, supplemented by crowdsourced annotations to label instances of cyberbullying. This approach leverages the vast, dynamic datasets, ensuring diversity in language and culture. Additionally, I would explore partnerships with platforms that offer API access to obtain structured data, ensuring compliance with privacy regulations such as GDPR. To address dataset imbalance, oversampling techniques can be applied to regions or biases that are underrepresented. Longitudinal surveys of adolescents would provide contextual user feedback, enhancing the dataset's relevance and ethical grounding.

    Which required skills will you need to have or develop for the chosen project?
    To successfully execute this project, I will need proficiency in understanding DL and LLM frameworks, including Python language if required for model development. Expertise in natural language processing (NLP) techniques, such as tokenisation and sentiment analysis, is essential for BERT fine-tuning. I should also develop skills in understanding multimodal data processing (e.g., image recognition with CNNs) and statistical analysis for evaluating metrics like F1-scores, drawing from renowned authors such as Binns (2017), Mehrabi et al. (2021), and Rosa et al. (2019). Ethical AI knowledge, including bias mitigation and privacy frameworks, is crucial, alongside collaboration skills for interdisciplinary teams that involve psychologists and policymakers. Developing a project management pipeline for the future roadmap will also be necessary as part of further recommendations.
