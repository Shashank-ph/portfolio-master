Unit 6 - Report Outline

  • Report Outline for Individual Essay by Shashank Phatak

    For the proposal, I shall be focusing on the advantages of an online shopping system (OSS) for grocery shops, the associated cyber threats and how to mitigate them.
    Proposed structure: 1. Introduction Briefly introduce the IT consultancy firm and the purpose of the engagement, which is to help the shop expand efficiently with a secure OSS while addressing cybersecurity concerns. As the director of a cyber security consultancy firm, I would propose the introduction of an Online Shopping System (OSS) to enhance the operational efficiency and scalability of the grocery store. Given the concerns about the rise in cybercrime and data protection regulations, we will integrate robust cybersecurity measures, including Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) and Anti-Malware tools, to ensure that the systems are secure. 2. Benefits of an Online Shopping System (OSS) 1. Operational Efficiency: OSS will automate inventory management, reduce manual errors, and optimise customer service, allowing for rapid scaling with the population growth. 2. Customer Convenience: An online store would allow customers to shop 24/7, increasing customer retention and reaching a wider audience. 3. Data Analytics: The system can gather valuable customer data to improve marketing strategies and manage stock appropriately. 4. Increased Efficiency: Automating inventory and order management while minimising human errors and streamlining operations. 5. Customer Accessibility: Enabling customers to place orders 24/7, expanding your reach beyond local walk-ins. 6. Growth Management: The automated system can scale quickly as the customer base grows, helping to maintain smooth operations. 3. Cybersecurity Concerns and Threats 1. Customer Data Breaches: A risk of data breaches where sensitive information such as payment details and addresses could be exposed. 2. Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks: As the business grows, the system may become a target for attacks that disrupt the shop's operations. 3. Malware and Ransomware: Threat actors could compromise the system, leading to loss of control or data encryption for ransom. 4. Unauthorized Access: Hackers could gain unauthorised access to the system, compromising sensitive business and customer data. 4. Threat Modelling Techniques 1. Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA): Enabling ZTA, which assumes that no entity (whether inside or outside the network) should be trusted by default. Any type of access is continuously evaluated based on strict identity verification, and every user or device is granted the least privilege necessary. 2. 4.2 Anti-Malware Tools: Anti-malware solutions are essential to protect the system from malicious software, such as viruses, spyware, and ransomware. 5. UML Diagrams: Preparing UML diagrams (Class / Abuse case or Sequence) to demonstrate how different actors like customers, administrators, and employees can interact with the Online Shopping System. 6. Mitigation Strategies o Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enhance security by requiring two or more forms of verification for accessing the system. o Regular Patching: Keeping software and anti-malware tools up to date to prevent exploitation of vulnerabilities. o Network Segmentation: Isolate sensitive systems and data to reduce the attack surface. o Backup and Disaster Recovery: Implement automatic backups to safeguard against ransomware attacks, ensuring business continuity. 7. Conclusion The proposed Online Shopping System will significantly improve the efficiency and scalability of the grocery business while providing customers with a convenient way to shop. By integrating Zero Trust Architecture and Anti-Malware tools, we can ensure that the system remains secure against emerging cyber threats
