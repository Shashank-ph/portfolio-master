Vulnerability Analysis – Literature Review Activity

  • Analysis of the PurpleSec Vulnerability Assessment Report Template


    Report Link: https://purplesec.us/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/Sample-Vulnerability-Assessment-Report-PurpleSec.pdf

    1. Does this template meet the NCSC-stated requirement of preparing a baseline to use as a reference point for pen tests?
    While the PurpleSec template is comprehensive and includes a thorough breakdown of identified vulnerabilities, their impact, and mitigation recommendations, it partially aligns with the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) guidance on establishing a baseline. It lacks explicit baseline metrics or documentation of an organization's current security posture against which future penetration tests (pen tests) could be measured.
    Proposed Changes/Amendments:
    • Add a "Baseline Metrics" Section: Include an overview of existing systems, configurations, and known risks before the assessment. Metrics could cover the number of systems scanned, known vulnerabilities, and prior incident history.
    • Include a History or Trend Analysis Section: Highlight recurring vulnerabilities or threats from previous assessments, providing context for tracking improvements or regressions.
    • Integrate Testing Scope Alignment: Clarify how this baseline aligns with pen testing goals, ensuring the focus remains relevant to specific threat vectors or compliance requirements.

    2. What are the two best lessons/examples presented in the report?
    • Clear Prioritization of Risks: The use of a risk matrix to classify vulnerabilities by severity and likelihood provides a straightforward method for prioritizing remediation efforts. This ensures that critical vulnerabilities receive immediate attention.
    • Detailed Remediation Guidance: The report includes practical and actionable steps to address each identified vulnerability, which is essential for organizations that may not have in-depth cybersecurity expertise.

    3. What two things are unnecessary or could be done more effectively?
    • Excessive Technical Jargon: Some sections rely on overly technical descriptions without sufficient explanation for non-technical stakeholders. Simplifying language or including a glossary could enhance accessibility.
    • Redundant Information in Executive Summary: The executive summary reiterates detailed findings already covered later in the report. Streamlining this section to focus solely on high-level insights and key recommendations would improve its impact.

    Security Threats and Vulnerabilities
    • Threats and Vulnerabilities Identified:
    The report demonstrates several common threats, such as open ports, outdated software, and misconfigurations in network systems. These vulnerabilities are consistent with risks identified using industry-standard methodologies.
    • Appropriate Tools and Techniques:
    Tools such as vulnerability scanners (e.g., Nessus, Qualys) and manual penetration testing techniques were employed to uncover and validate findings. Including methodologies like OWASP for web applications and adherence to standards such as ISO 27001/27002 ensures a systematic approach to risk identification and mitigation.

    • Recommendations:
    To manage these vulnerabilities, it is crucial to:
    1. Implement a patch management policy to address outdated systems.
    2. Harden network configurations, particularly for public-facing assets.
    3. Regularly review and update user access privileges.

    Reflection
    Did you have any issues or challenges with this activity?
    Understanding the connection between vulnerability assessment and the establishment of a baseline was initially challenging. The report's lack of explicit baseline data required additional research into NCSC guidelines to fill the gap.

    How did you overcome them?
    I referred to supplementary resources on NCSC standards and compared these with other vulnerability assessment templates to understand best practices for baseline reporting.

    How will they affect your final report?
    These insights will help refine the structure of my final report to ensure it explicitly includes baseline metrics, making it a robust reference point for pen testing. I will also focus on clear communication and actionable recommendations tailored to varied stakeholders.
